Treating stomach ache at home

Speak to your pharmacist about medicine to help with:

  • feeling bloated (trapped wind)
  • indigestion (burning pain after eating, maybe bringing up food)
  • constipation (can’t poo)

You might also vomit or have diarrhoea with your stomach ache. This will usually go away after a few days.

You should drink a lot of fluids. Things like water mixed with fruit juice or soup broth are good. You can tell if you’re drinking enough by checking your urine. It should be light yellow or look like water.

Painkillers like paracetamol or ibuprofen can help with the pain but won’t stop the diarrhoea or vomiting.

When to see a GP

  • the pain gets much worse in a short space of time
  • the pain or bloating won’t go away or keeps coming back
  • you have other symptoms like weight loss or vomiting
  • you’ve had your stomach ache for a while
Book a GP appointment

Call 999 or go to A&E if you:

  • have sudden, severe stomach pain
  • have pain when you touch your stomach
  • are vomiting blood or a ground coffee like substance
  • have bloody or black, sticky poo
  • have collapsed
  • can’t breathe
  • unable to pee
  • are diabetic and vomiting

Still not sure what to do?

If you need help deciding what to do next, telephone 111 or visit 111 online.
