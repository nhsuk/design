Call 999 if you have any of these:

  • sudden, bad stomach pain
  • pain when you touch your stomach
  • vomiting blood
  • blood in your poo
  • you collapsed
  • can’t breathe
  • not peeing at all
  • you have an ongoing serious illness
  • you’re diabetic

When to see a GP

  • the pain gets much worse in a short space of time
  • the pain won't go away
  • you also have other symptoms like loss of weight, vomiting, or abnormal bleeding
Book a GP appointment

Treating stomach ache at home

Your pharmacist can give you medicine to help with:

  • feeling bloated
  • indigestion - feeling full, heavy, sick, maybe bringing up food
  • constipation

There are also things you can do yourself to help with bloating, indigestion and constipation

Vomiting and diarrhoea

You might also vomit or have with your stomach ache.

There are things you can do yourself to help with vomiting and diarrhoea.

This isn’t usually anything serious and will go away after a few days. If it doesn’t or you feel much worse, you should speak to your GP
