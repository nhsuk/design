Treating stomach ache at home

Speak to your pharmacist about medicine to help with:

  • feeling bloated (trapped wind)
  • indigestion (feeling full, sick, maybe bringing up food)
  • constipation (can’t poo)

There are also things you can do yourself to help with bloating, indigestion and constipation

When to see a GP:

  • the pain gets much worse in a short space of time
  • the pain or bloating won’t go away or keeps coming back
  • you have other symptoms like weight loss or vomiting
Book a GP appointment

Vomiting and diarrhoea

You might also vomit or have diarrhoea with your stomach ache.

This isn’t usually anything serious and will go away after a few days.

There are things you can do yourself to help with vomiting and diarrhoea.

Call 999 or go to A&E now if you:

  • have sudden, bad stomach pain
  • have pain when you touch your stomach
  • are vomiting blood
  • have blood in your poo
  • have collapsed
  • can’t breathe
  • are not peeing at all
  • have an ongoing serious illness
  • are diabetic

Still not sure what to do?

If you need help deciding what to do next, telephone 111 or visit 111 online.
